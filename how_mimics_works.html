The science

Why Cell Mimics
Replace Cells

Through the intersection of biochemistry, polymer chemistry and high-precision manufacturing, we have created synthetic cell mimics that behave similarly to real cells. The Slingshot platform is the world’s first on demand, synthetic cell design platform to address the industry’s reliance on and limitations around biologically-derived cellular controls.

This breakthrough technology creates synthetic cell mimics that match the optical, fluorescence and biochemical features of biological cells. The result is the ideal cell control, achieved without sacrificing performance or consistency.

Synthetic cell mimics are shelf stable and can be created on-demand and at scale. Slingshot’s technology decreases costs, shortens development timelines and increases accessibility to cell-like controls. Our cutting-edge technology platform can be used in a range of applications including:

01 — Assay Development
Standardize across every stage of development

Engineer custom controls for biomarker assays, immune monitoring, and novel endpoints. Move assays from translational research through pivotal trials without operator variability or lot-to-lot drift.

02 — Laboratory Diagnostics
Model any disease state, not just common ones

Match biomarker expression to specific disease states, including rare and emerging indications where commercial reference material doesn’t exist. Tune antigen density to model severity — not just presence.

03 — Adoptive Cell Therapy
Controls cell therapy manufacturing actually needs

Validate CAR-T potency, identity, and purity endpoints with custom controls for enumeration, transgene expression, and viral vector characterization — even when no commercial reference exists.

04 — Drug Development
Consistent data from Phase 1 through approval

Stable, reproducible reagents reduce complex normalisation workflows historically required for multi-site clinical trials. Phase 1 to NDA: same signal, same control, same lot.

05 — Instrument Performance
Tech errors visible against a stable baseline

Calibration, compensation, and unmixing controls for cross-platform consistency. Instrument drift stands out immediately when your reference is a synthetic standard, not a variable biological.
